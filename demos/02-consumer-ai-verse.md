# Verse — AI Lyricist for Indie Musicians

## Overview
Verse is an AI songwriting copilot for indie musicians and bedroom producers. You hum a melody, describe the mood ("late-night, bittersweet, about leaving a small town"), and Verse returns three complete lyric drafts in your voice — verse, chorus, bridge — matched to your melody's phrasing and meter. We're targeting the ~12 million indie artists on Spotify and the ~3 million active users of DAWs like Ableton, FL Studio, and Logic.

## The insight
Every musician we've talked to writes instrumentals fast and gets stuck on lyrics. They'll sit on a finished beat for weeks because the lyrics won't come. Existing tools (RhymeZone, Masterpiece Studio) solve 10% of the problem. ChatGPT writes bad, generic lyrics. Nobody has built a lyrics tool that actually understands meter, rhyme, and personal voice.

## Product
Native macOS and iPad app. Real-time melody-to-lyric generation. Integrates with Ableton and Logic via a plugin. Users can "teach" Verse their voice by uploading 3-5 songs they've written, and subsequent drafts inherit their vocabulary, imagery, and cadence.

## Pricing
Freemium. Free tier gets 10 lyric drafts/month. Pro tier $14/month for unlimited drafts and voice training. We've benchmarked against Splice ($13/mo), Output Arcade ($10/mo), and LANDR ($10/mo) — these are the pricing anchors our users are already used to.

## Distribution
Our GTM is influencer-first. We're partnering with 20 YouTube music producers (50K-500K subs each) for paid demos. We're also running a closed beta on Reddit r/WeAreTheMusicMakers and r/edmproduction — those two subs alone are 1.5M subscribers, and a product demo that works will go viral there.

## Defensibility
Our voice-modeling approach is proprietary: we fine-tune a smaller model per user on their own lyrics, not just prompt-engineer. This means the product gets measurably better the more you use it, and switching costs grow with every session. We've filed a provisional patent on the technique.
